# Legal Compliance Guide: AI Phone Calls and SMS in the United States (2026)

## 1. FCC Rules on AI-Generated Calls

### February 2024 Declaratory Ruling (FCC 24-17)
The FCC unanimously ruled that AI-generated voices qualify as "artificial or prerecorded voices" under the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA), 47 U.S.C. Section 227. This means:

- **All calls using AI-generated voices require prior express consent** from the called party before the call is made.
- For **telemarketing** calls, prior express **written** consent is required.
- For **informational** (non-marketing) calls, prior express **oral** consent suffices.
- The FCC can fine violators, order carrier blocking, and victims can sue for damages.

### July 2024 NPRM (Further Rulemaking)
The FCC issued a Notice of Proposed Rulemaking proposing:
- A formal definition of "AI-generated call"
- A requirement for **in-call disclosure** that AI is being used
- Specific consent requirements for AI calls beyond existing TCPA consent

As of March 2026, the final rule from this NPRM has not yet been adopted, but it signals the direction of regulation.

### October 2025 FNPRM (Deregulation Proposal)
Under the current FCC, a Further Notice of Proposed Rulemaking proposes **streamlining** certain TCPA/DNC rules:
- Potentially **eliminating the "revoke all" rule** (where opt-out of one channel = opt-out of all channels)
- Potentially **eliminating company-specific DNC lists** (relying on the National DNC Registry instead)
- Modernizing caller ID requirements to mandate a working, verifiable callback number
- 60-day comment period followed the October 28, 2025 open meeting

**Bottom line**: The Feb 2024 ruling that AI voices = artificial voices under TCPA is settled law. Additional AI-specific rules are pending. The regulatory direction may be slightly more business-friendly under the current FCC, but the core TCPA obligations for AI calls remain fully in effect.

## 3. FCC One-to-One Consent Rule: DEAD

### What Happened
- The FCC adopted a "one-to-one consent" rule requiring that TCPA consent could only be given to a single, specific seller (not shared via lead generators).
- **January 24, 2025**: The Eleventh Circuit vacated the rule in *Insurance Marketing Coalition v. FCC*, holding the FCC exceeded its statutory authority. The court ruled the TCPA only requires "clear and unmistakable" consent, not the additional constraints the FCC tried to impose.
- **April 2025**: The FCC announced it would not challenge the ruling.
- **September 2025**: The FCC formally deleted the vacated language and reinstated the prior version of the rules.

### Current State
The one-to-one consent rule is **officially dead**. Consent can still be shared through lead generators and affiliated parties, provided the consumer "clearly and unmistakably" consents to receive calls. However, this does not eliminate the need for **prior express written consent** for telemarketing calls -- it just means that consent need not be seller-specific.

## 4. B2B Calling Exemptions

### What IS Exempt
- **Calls to business landlines** are exempt from TCPA do-not-call list requirements
- **Calls to business landlines** are exempt from the TCPA's autodialer restrictions
- B2B calls are exempt from some provisions of the FTC's Telemarketing Sales Rule (TSR)

### What IS NOT Exempt (Critical)
- **Calls to mobile phones** are NEVER exempt, even if the phone is used for business. The TCPA protects the device, not the person. A business owner's cell phone has the same TCPA protections as any consumer's.
- **Mixed-use phones** are presumptively residential under FCC interpretation
- **Mobile numbers on the National DNC Registry** are treated as residential regardless of business use
- **States with no B2B exemptions**: Florida, Maryland, Oklahoma, and Washington have implemented stricter telemarketing laws with no B2B carve-outs
- Using an **ATDS (Automatic Telephone Dialing System)** to call any wireless number requires prior express written consent, regardless of whether the call is B2B

### Practical Impact for CondomX
When dispatching to subcontractors, if you are calling their **mobile phones** (which most contractors use), you cannot rely on any B2B exemption. You need consent. The safest approach is to obtain written consent during the subcontractor onboarding process.

**Critical for AI calls**: If your AI agent records calls (for quality, training, or dispute resolution), you MUST obtain consent from the called party in all two-party states. The AI's disclosure at the start of the call should include a recording notice.

## 6. A2P 10DLC Enforcement

### Current Status (2026)
- **As of February 1, 2025**: All major US carriers (T-Mobile, AT&T, Verizon) block **100% of unregistered 10DLC traffic**. Not throttled -- blocked entirely.
- Unregistered numbers may also be **permanently blacklisted** for repeated violations.

### Registration Process and Costs

| Item | Cost | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Brand registration (TCR) | $4.50 | Increased from $4.00 as of Aug 2025 |
| Standard brand vetting | $41.50 | Increased from $40.00 |
| Campaign verification | $15.00 | Per campaign |
| T-Mobile pass-through (SMS) | $0.0025/msg | Effective Jan 19, 2026 |

### Non-Compliance Fines (T-Mobile Tiers)
- **Tier 1** (phishing/smishing): $2,000/violation
- **Tier 2** (illegal content): $1,000/violation
- **Tier 3** (other violations including SHAFT content): $500/violation
- General: Up to **$10,000 per violation** for non-compliance

### Timeline
Registration typically takes 1-4 weeks for brand registration and vetting, plus additional time for campaign approval. Plan for 4-6 weeks total before sending any A2P messages.

## 8. Best Practices for AI Disclosure

Based on how successful AI calling companies handle compliance:

### Opening Disclosure Script (Best Practice)
```
"Hello, this is [AI Agent Name], an AI assistant calling on behalf of 
[Company Name]. This call may be recorded for quality purposes. 
I'm calling regarding [purpose]. You can ask to speak with a human 
at any time, or say 'stop' to end this call. May I continue?"
```

### Key Elements to Include
1. **AI identity**: State it is an AI / not a live person within the first 30 seconds (required in CA, TX)
2. **Company identification**: Name the business the AI represents
3. **Purpose of the call**: State why you're calling
4. **Recording disclosure**: If recording, disclose this (required in 13 states)
5. **Human transfer option**: Offer transfer to a live agent at any time (required in TX)
6. **Opt-out mechanism**: Explain how to stop the call or future calls

### Operational Best Practices
- Log every disclosure with timestamp in call metadata
- Implement real-time compliance monitoring
- Use multi-factor authentication and encryption for consent data
- Maintain audit trails showing disclosure was delivered before any substantive content
- Train AI to detect and honor opt-out language in natural conversation (not just keywords)
- Allow revocation by "any reasonable means" per April 2025 FCC rule

## 10. Insurance Requirements for Subcontractors

### What Dispatchers Must Verify

**Non-Negotiable Coverage:**

| Coverage Type | Minimum (Standard) | 2025-2026 Trend |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| General Liability | $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate | Trending to $2M per occurrence |
| Workers' Compensation | State statutory limits | Required in all states (with narrow sole-proprietor exemptions) |
| Commercial Auto | $1M combined single limit | Required if using vehicles for work |
| Professional Liability / E&O | $1M per occurrence | Recommended for specialized trades |
| Umbrella/Excess | $1M-$5M | Increasingly required by property managers |

**Critical Endorsements to Verify:**
- **Additional Insured endorsement**: Must name your company (the dispatcher) as additional insured on the sub's GL policy
- **Waiver of Subrogation**: Prevents the sub's insurer from suing you after paying a claim
- **Primary and Non-Contributory**: Ensures the sub's policy pays first, before yours

**Verification Process:**
- Collect Certificates of Insurance (COI) **before any work begins**
- Verify the actual endorsements are attached (not just listed on the certificate)
- Monitor for continuous coverage -- policies can lapse
- Use automated COI tracking (services like myCOI, Evident, BCS)
- Re-verify annually at minimum, quarterly recommended

**Dispatcher Liability**: If a subcontractor causes damage or injury and is uninsured or underinsured, the dispatcher/general contractor can be held liable. The additional insured endorsement is the primary protection mechanism.

## 12. Consent Management Best Practices

### What to Record (Per 16 CFR Section 310.5 / TSR Recordkeeping)
Every consent record must include:
1. **Full name** of the person providing consent
2. **Telephone number** consented for contact
3. **Exact copy** of the consent request as presented to the consumer
4. **Purpose** for which consent was given
5. **Copy of the consent** as provided (screenshot, form capture, audio recording)
6. **Date and time** consent was given
7. **Method of acquisition** (web form, verbal, paper, SMS)
8. **IP address** (for web-based consent)
9. **Any additional context** specific to billing or the interaction

### Retention Requirements
- **Minimum 5 years** for consent documentation (TSR requirement)
- **Virginia SB 1339**: Opt-out requests must be honored for **10 years**
- **Practical recommendation**: Retain consent records for the **statute of limitations period plus buffer** -- typically 4 years for TCPA (varies by state) plus 1-2 years margin. Five years minimum, 7-10 years recommended.

### Revocation Processing (Post-April 2025)
- Consumers can revoke by **any reasonable means** (phone, text, email, verbal, web form)
- Must process within **10 business days** of revocation request
- Must cease **all** calls and texts across **all channels** (current rule; "revoke all" provision delayed to April 2026 and under review)
- Document the revocation request with timestamp and method

### Technical Implementation
- **Immutable audit logs**: Every consent grant and revocation with timestamps
- **Encryption at rest and in transit** for all consent data
- **Automated DNC list scrubbing** before every outbound campaign
- **Real-time opt-out processing** integrated into the calling system
- **Regular compliance audits** with documented results
- **Staff training documentation** with records of completion

For an AI-powered facility maintenance dispatcher making calls and sending SMS to subcontractors:

1. **Obtain written consent during subcontractor onboarding** -- covers TCPA, state mini-TCPAs, and recording consent
2. **AI disclosure at call start** -- "I am an AI calling on behalf of [Company]" within 30 seconds
3. **Recording disclosure** -- if recording, disclose before substantive conversation begins
4. **Human transfer option** -- always available (required in TX, best practice everywhere)
5. **A2P 10DLC registration** -- mandatory before sending any SMS; budget 4-6 weeks
6. **DNC scrubbing** -- check National Registry before every outbound call/text campaign
7. **Consent records** -- digital, timestamped, encrypted, retained 5+ years
8. **Revocation processing** -- honor within 10 business days, any reasonable means
9. **Insurance verification** -- COI with additional insured endorsement before dispatching any sub
10. **State-specific compliance** -- extra requirements in CA, TX, FL, VA, CO; no B2B exemption in FL, MD, OK, WA
11. **Caller ID** -- working, verifiable callback number identifying your company
12. **Audit trail** -- log every call, disclosure, consent check, and opt-out for litigation defense
