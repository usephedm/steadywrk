# Technician Recruitment, Network Building, and Scoring for Dispatch -- 2026

## 1. Where Facility Maintenance Technicians Look for Work

Technicians use a **multi-platform strategy** to find work. No single channel dominates.

**Job Boards (Employment-style)**
- **Indeed** -- The largest job board for maintenance technician roles. Employers post full-time and contract positions. Active across all US markets with robust search by trade and location.
- **Craigslist** -- Still heavily used, especially for local/regional maintenance work. Active listings across every major metro (NYC, Denver, Seattle, Orlando, San Diego). Low barrier to entry, no fees for job seekers. Attracts both W-2 and 1099 workers.
- **ZipRecruiter** -- Growing presence for facility maintenance roles, particularly corporate/enterprise positions.

**Gig/Lead Platforms (Contractor-style)**
- **Thumbtack** -- Charges contractors $15-$60+ per lead (even if they don't win the job). Allows both licensed contractors and skilled handymen. Large volume of residential and light commercial work.
- **TaskRabbit** -- On-demand marketplace for immediate, same-day help. One-time $25 registration fee, then 15-30% commission per completed job. Strongest for small-to-medium tasks.
- **Angi (formerly Angie's List/HomeAdvisor)** -- $10-$50 per lead plus 10-30% referral fee. Optional $300+/year membership for visibility boost. Best for major home improvement and maintenance projects.
- **Handy** -- Focused on cleaning and handyman services. Lower-skill threshold.

**Social/Community Channels**
- **Facebook Groups** -- Trade-specific groups (e.g., "HVAC Techs Looking for Work", local handyman groups) are significant recruitment channels. Organic, trust-based, zero cost.
- **Nextdoor** -- Hyperlocal social network where businesses create Business Pages and are discovered through neighbor recommendations. Organic referral-driven, no lead fees. High trust signal.

**Emerging**
- **GigNGo** -- New platform (2026) specifically targeting handyman gig work with zero commissions.
- **Jobber** -- While primarily FSM software, their academy content documents that the most successful contractors use 3-5 platforms simultaneously.

**Key Insight for CondomX**: The technicians you want are scattered across all these platforms. A recruitment strategy must be multi-channel. The highest-quality independent techs tend to prefer platforms with lower fees (Nextdoor, Facebook, Craigslist) and resist high-commission platforms (Thumbtack, Angi) unless volume justifies it.

## 4. Technician Scoring Systems

**How Major Platforms Score Workers**

| Platform | Rating Type | Mechanism | Consequence |
|----------|------------|-----------|-------------|
| Uber | Averaged (1-5 stars) | Rolling average across all trips | Below 4.6 triggers warnings; deactivation threshold varies by market |
| DoorDash | Averaged composite | Customer rating, completion rate, on-time rate | Below 4.2 customer rating risks deactivation |
| TaskRabbit | Incremental (per-review) | Every individual review score visible | Low scores reduce search ranking |
| Corrigo | Overall score | Client ratings visible to all potential clients | Low scores reduce new work offers |

**Research Findings (HBR/EDHEC/Stanford)**
- **Averaged scores** (Uber, DoorDash) smooth out anomalies and foster long-term loyalty -- a single bad day doesn't destroy your standing.
- **Incremental scores** (TaskRabbit) provide transparency but make workers more anxious about each individual job.
- Workers are poor at estimating individual scores when only shown averages. A driver with two 5-star and one 1-star rating (avg 3.67) significantly overestimates their most recent score.
- **Algorithmic opacity** is a major issue: all major platforms except Amazon Flex use opaque, ever-changing algorithms that keep workers uncertain about how pay and visibility are calculated.
- Stanford research (Aug 2025) found Uber's rating system effectively **steers drivers toward better performance** through nudges rather than punishment.

**Earnings & Algorithmic Control**
- Earnings are frequently controlled by algorithmic systems that regulate job request frequency/profitability based on compliance with performance metrics (acceptance rate, customer satisfaction, on-time delivery).
- Human Rights Watch (May 2025) documented this as "algorithmic wage and labor exploitation."

**Key Insight for CondomX**: Build a **composite score** with transparent weighting. Recommended dimensions:
- **Reliability** (show-up rate, on-time arrival) -- 30%
- **Quality** (client rating, first-time fix rate) -- 30%
- **Responsiveness** (acceptance speed, communication) -- 20%
- **Compliance** (license current, insurance valid, background check) -- 20%

Transparency is the competitive advantage. Show techs exactly how their score is calculated and what affects it. This directly addresses the #1 gig worker complaint (algorithmic opacity).

Sources: [HBR - How Ratings Shape Behavior](https://hbr.org/2024/04/research-how-ratings-systems-shape-user-behavior-in-the-gig-economy), [Stanford - How Uber Steers Drivers](https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2025/08/uber-ride-sharing-quality-control-ratings-incentive-system-research), [HRW - The Gig Trap](https://www.hrw.org/report/2025/05/12/the-gig-trap/algorithmic-wage-and-labor-exploitation-in-platform-work-in-the-us), [EDHEC - Ratings in Gig Economy](https://www.edhec.edu/en/research-and-faculty/edhec-vox/how-ratings-systems-shape-user-behavior-gig-economy)

## 6. Geospatial Matching (H3 / PostGIS / Geohashing)

**Uber's H3 System -- The Gold Standard**

H3 is Uber's open-source hierarchical hexagonal global grid system. It is the proven approach for dispatch matching at scale.

**Why hexagons over squares:**
- Hexagons have **one distance** between a centerpoint and all neighbors (squares have two, triangles have three).
- Six neighbors at equal distance makes movement, clustering, and density calculations smoother.
- Better approximation of circular search radii.

**How H3 dispatch works:**
1. Every location (tech, job site) is converted to an H3 index (64-bit identifier encoding resolution + location + hierarchy).
2. To find nearby techs: use `kRing`/`gridDisk` function to expand outward from the job's hexagon, finding all hexagons within k steps.
3. **Bipartite matching**: riders and drivers (or jobs and techs) form two node sets in a graph. The algorithm finds optimal edge assignments using heuristics and approximation algorithms (not brute-force Hungarian algorithm, which is too slow at scale).
4. Multi-objective optimization balances: wait time, trip distance, tech skill match, cost, and availability.

**Resolution Levels for CondomX:**
- Resolution 7 (~5.16 km^2 per hex) -- good for urban dispatch zones
- Resolution 8 (~0.74 km^2 per hex) -- good for dense urban areas
- Resolution 9 (~0.10 km^2 per hex) -- precise neighborhood matching

**Tech Stack:**
- Uber's production stack: Apache Kafka (streaming), Flink/Spark (real-time processing), Cassandra/Redis (operational data), H3 (spatial indexing).
- For CondomX at smaller scale: **PostGIS + H3 extension** is sufficient. PostgreSQL with PostGIS supports H3 natively via `h3-pg` extension. This avoids the complexity of Uber's distributed architecture while getting the same spatial indexing benefits.

**Key Insight for CondomX**: Use H3 at resolution 7-8 for zone definition (these become your "Smart Zones" like Corrigo). Store tech home locations and real-time positions as H3 indices. Matching becomes a simple index lookup + k-ring expansion, which is O(1) per cell vs O(n) for brute-force distance calculations.

## 10. Referral Networks (Tech-Refers-Tech)

**Best Practices from Research:**

- **Double-sided incentives beat single-sided by 2x.** Both the referrer and the referred must receive a reward. Example: referrer gets $50, new tech gets $25 upon completing first job.
- **Cash/gift cards outperform service credits.** A $50 Amazon gift card generates more referrals than "$50 off platform fees" because it has immediate, tangible value.
- **Referral cost per acquisition should be 50-70% lower than paid advertising.** If not, the incentive structure is too generous or referral quality is too low.
- **Milestone-based payouts** work well: referral bonus awarded after the referred tech completes a threshold (e.g., 5 jobs or stays active for 30 days). This prevents gaming.
- **Technician-driven referrals**: some platforms give techs a cut of referral rewards. If a tech-driven referral generates a job, the tech gets $25 and the customer gets $50.

**Platform Examples:**
- **Locorum** -- General contracting referral platform specifically for contractor-to-contractor referrals
- **Openforce** -- 190,000 active contractors on platform, referral-driven network growth
